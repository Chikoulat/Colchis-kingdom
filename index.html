About the Kingdom

Colchis was an ancient kingdom located on the eastern coast of the Black Sea in what is now modern-day Georgia. The kingdom is believed to have been founded in the 13th century BCE and was known for its wealth and advanced civilization. Colchis was famous for its production of gold, silver, and other precious metals, as well as for its agricultural products, including grapes, nuts, and fruits. The kingdom was also known for its advanced culture, which included a system of writing, architecture, and art. One of the most famous myths associated with Colchis is the story of Jason and the Argonauts. According to legend, the kingdom was ruled by King Aeetes, who possessed the Golden Fleece. Jason and his crew of Argonauts set out on a quest to find the Golden Fleece and, after many trials and tribulations, succeeded in obtaining it. The kingdom of Colchis was eventually conquered by the Persians in the 6th century BCE and later fell under the rule of various empires, including the Roman and Byzantine Empires. Today, the region that was once Colchis is part of modern-day Georgia.
